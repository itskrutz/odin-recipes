Poha Recipe

Description:

Poha is a beloved Indian breakfast dish known for its simplicity and delicious flavor. It's a popular choice across India, with regional variations that add unique twists to the recipe. Poha is made from flattened rice, which is lightweight and easy to prepare. This dish is not only a quick and nutritious breakfast option but also a perfect example of the diverse and flavorful cuisine found in India.

Poha is often prepared with a variety of seasonings and ingredients, making it a versatile and customizable dish. Whether you like it spicy or mildly flavored, Poha can be adapted to suit your taste. It's commonly garnished with fresh coriander leaves, grated coconut, and a dash of lemon juice, adding a burst of freshness to each bite. In addition to being a delightful breakfast, Poha can be enjoyed as a light snack or even as a quick and satisfying dinner option. Its preparation is relatively simple, making it an ideal choice for those looking for a delicious and nutritious meal in a hurry.

Ingredients:

  • Poha (flattened rice)
  • Oil (usually vegetable oil)
  • Mustard seeds
  • Cumin seeds
  • Curry leaves
  • Green chilies
  • Onions
  • Potatoes (optional)
  • Peas (optional)
  • Turmeric powder
  • Salt
  • Sugar (optional)
  • Lemon juice
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Grated coconut (optional)

Steps to Make Poha:

  1. Rinse the Poha thoroughly under running water in a sieve or colander. Gently wash it and let it sit for a few minutes to soak. It should become soft but not mushy.
  2. In a pan, heat oil on medium heat. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Allow them to splutter.
  3. Add curry leaves, green chilies, and chopped onions to the pan. Saut√© until the onions turn translucent.
  4. If you're using them, add finely chopped potatoes and peas to the mixture. Cook until they become tender.
  5. Sprinkle turmeric powder and salt over the mixture. You can also add a bit of sugar if you prefer a slightly sweet taste.
  6. Add the rinsed Poha to the pan and gently mix it with the spices and vegetables. Cook for a few minutes until it's heated through and well-coated with the spices.
  7. If you like, sprinkle a little lemon juice over the Poha for a tangy flavor. Mix well.
  8. Garnish the Poha with fresh coriander leaves and grated coconut if desired.
  9. Serve your delicious Poha hot, with some extra lemon wedges and garnishes on the side.
